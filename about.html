Fast fashion moves pieces from factory to landfill in eighteen months. We move them from one careful owner to another — and they get better with every decade they survive.

We don't list anything new. We don't list replicas. We don't list anything we couldn't tell you the story of. If a piece doesn't pass verification, it doesn't go live. Full stop.

01

Why we built this.

Vintage resale has a trust problem. Too many platforms let anything through — "vintage-inspired," fast fashion rebranded as retro, pieces with no provenance and no accountability. Buyers get burned. Good pieces get buried.

Nunc started from a single conviction: that the archive deserves better. That a genuine 1974 leather bomber shouldn't have to compete with a factory replica. That buyers should be able to trust what they see, and sellers should be able to reach buyers who actually care.

Every piece we turn down is a line we hold. The day we list something that doesn't belong here is the day we become what we're trying to fix.
— the nunc team, est. 2026
02

How verification works.

Every piece goes through a three-stage process before it ever goes live. No shortcuts. We'd rather turn a piece away than let something through that we're not sure of.

Step 01
Intake & photography
Seller uploads photos of the item, the label, and the care tag. We flag anything that looks off before human review begins.
AI-assisted · immediate
Step 02
Human authentication
A Nunc verifier reviews age indicators, construction quality, label typography, and stitching. Era, fabric, and condition are confirmed.
Human review · 24–48h
Step 03
Listed & logged
The piece goes live with its full record attached — era, condition grade, fabric composition, provenance note. Permanent and public.
Permanent record

04

Honest condition. Always.

We grade every piece on a six-point scale, from New with Tags down to Already Mended. We don't hide wear — we describe it, price for it, and log it.

A piece graded Loved isn't a lesser piece. It's a piece with a history you can see. A piece graded Already Mended has been cared for — the repair is documented, the mender is credited. We think that's worth more than pretending it never happened.

No grade is shameful. The only shameful thing would be calling a Loved piece Excellent and hoping no one notices.
